
## Statement
> [!theorem] Theorem: Weak law of large numbers Ver.1
> Let $X_1, X_2, \dots$ be i.i.d. with
> $$
> \begin{aligned}
> x \operatorname{P}(|X_i| > x) \to 0 \quad \text{as } x \to \infty
> \end{aligned}
> $$
> Let $S_n = X_1 + \dots + X_n$ and let $\mu_n = \operatorname{E}(X_1 1_{(|X_1| \le n)})$. Then $S_n/n - \mu_n \to 0$ in probability.

## Khinchin's Version
> [!theorem] Theorem: Weak law of large numbers Ver.2
> Let $X_1, X_2, \dots$ be i.i.d. with $E|X_i| < \infty$. Let $S_n = X_1 + \cdots + X_n$ and let $\mu = E X_1$. Then $S_n/n \to \mu$ in probability.
