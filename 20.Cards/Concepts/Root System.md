
## Preliminaries

* [[Reflection and Reflecting Hyperplane]]
## Statement

> [!definition] Definition:
> A subset $R$ of a real vector space $E$ is a **root system** if it satisfies the following axioms.
>
> (R1) $R$ is finite, it spans $E$, and it does not contain $0$.
>
> (R2) If $\alpha\in R$, then the only scalar multiples of $\alpha$ in $R$ are $\pm\alpha$.
>
> (R3) If $\alpha\in R$, then the reflection $s_\alpha$ permutes the elements of $R$.
>
> (R4) If $\alpha,\beta\in R$, then $\langle\beta,\alpha\rangle\in\mathbf{Z}$.
>
> The elements of $R$ are called **roots**.


## Examples

> [!example] Example: 
> We work in $\mathbb{R}^{\ell+ 1}$ , with the Euclidean inner product.
> Define
>  $$ R: =\left\{ \pm \left(\varepsilon _{i}-\varepsilon _{j}\right): 1\le i< j\le \ell+ 1 \right\} $$ 
> and Let $E =\operatorname{span}R =\left\{ \sum\alpha_{i}\varepsilon _{i}:\sum\alpha_{i} =0 \right\}$. Then $R$ is a root system in $E$
