
## Definition

> [!definition] Definition: Gamma Distribution
> Let $\alpha > 0$ (shape parameter) and $\lambda > 0$ (rate parameter). A random variable $X$ is said to follow a **Gamma distribution** with parameters $(\alpha, \lambda)$, denoted as $X \sim \Gamma(\alpha, \lambda)$. If the probability density function is
> $$\begin{cases}
> \frac{\lambda^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)} x^{\alpha-1} e^{-\lambda x} & \text{if } x > 0, \\
> 0 & \text{otherwise},
> \end{cases}$$
> where $\Gamma(\alpha) = \int_0^\infty z^{\alpha-1}e^{-z}dz$ is the Gamma function.

## Characteristic Function

> [!proposition] Proposition: 
> The characteristic function of $X\sim \Gamma\left(\alpha ,\beta \right)$ is   
> $$ \varphi_{X}\left(t\right)=\left(\frac{\beta  }{\beta -it }\right)^{\alpha }=  \left(1- \frac{it }{\beta  }\right)^{-\alpha } $$
